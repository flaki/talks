Conventions

What are Conventions?

A pattern of behaviour that is customary, expected and self-enforcing.

Economic importance


Effectively reduces transaction costs:

  • Out of a host of conceivable choices, only one is used.
  • Resolves indeterminacy in interactions of multiple equilibria.
  • The equilibria of multiple equilibriums - the equilibrium that everyone expects.

How do conventions arise?


Conventions are established:

  • by a central authority
  • by gradual accretion of precedent


Note, that these are not neccessarily mutually exclusive.

The paper


  • Emerging of conventions by gradual accretion of precedent
  • An evolutionary "bottom up" model is used
  • Game theoretic principles, but with bounded rationality

An Evolutionary Model
of Convention Formation

Basic rules

  • Fixed rules & strategies
  • Local interactions - players drawn from large population
  • Periodical one-shot games between the population

Bounded rationality

The model is built on realistic principles of:

  • Bounded rationality
  • Partial information only
  • Agents with limited foresight
  • A limited history
    ("remembering" a limited number of periods)


Players act "rules of thumb"
(based on personal experience and information from other agents)

Random perturbations


  • Most of the time agents act like described before
  • Rarerly, but occasionally they act randomly
  • Random perturbations - small, inexplicable changes, mimicing evolution
  • These most of the time have explanations, but outside the model principles


"stochastic shocks"

Path dependency


Two society with the same initial conditions might end up in two completely different states at the same time

Ergodic process


Societies change regularly, and spend a statistically predictable percentage of the time in given states, regardless of the respective inital conditions.

Local conformity

Most of the time the population will in equilibrium (=using the same convention).

  • All agents have a chance to interact with each other
  • Sufficiently incomplete information
  • Small prevalence of random perturbations

Global diversity


  • Arises when interactions of societies are severely limited
  • Communities probably end up using different conventions
  • Even if started out with the same initial conditions

Distributive Bargaining

Sharecropping


  • Bargaining situations between landlord and tenant.
  • Agreed-upon terms (contract) of the crop output
  • Suprisingly prevalent distribution of 50/50
  • Regardless of the work spent on the crop differs
  • Regardless of the type of crop or land quality

Equilibrium


The 50/50 is not just a fair sulution, but the most robust one


It is proven, that the 25/75 -> 50/50 transition is more probable (takes less random perturbations) than the opposite one - it is stochastically stable

Conclusion

Conclusion

  • Focal points help making sense of real world events
  • Studying expectation formation is possible
  • Certain norms are more prevalent because they are
    more stable (and not because they are more natural or ethical)


The models used realistic assumptions (no perfect information, no prescience)
